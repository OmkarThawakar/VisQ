Common Questions

  • VisQ needs photo-library permission to index and search media stored on your device.
  • Indexing and retrieval are intended to run locally on device rather than through a remote backend.
  • VisQ is intended to work with photos and videos from the Apple Photos library that you choose to let the app access.
  • The core experience does not require a separate user account based on the current implementation.
  • Initial indexing may take time for larger libraries and may require free storage for local app data.
  • If access was denied, open iOS Settings and update the Photos permission for VisQ.
  • You can remove locally stored app data by deleting the app from your device.

Troubleshooting

If the app is not showing results, first verify that Photos access is granted and that at least some photos or videos have been indexed.
  • Check iOS Settings and confirm VisQ has photo-library access.
  • Allow the first indexing run to finish before expecting complete search results.
  • Try indexing a smaller set of photos or videos first if the initial batch is very large.
  • Keep enough free space on the device for local embeddings, descriptions, and related app data.
  • Restart the app after changing Photos permissions or after installing a new app version.
