How VisQ Handles Data

This Privacy Policy explains how the VisQ iOS app handles information when you install and use the app.

Last updated: April 3, 2026

Overview

VisQ is designed to perform photo and video retrieval on device. Based on the current app implementation, the app does not require account creation and does not appear to send your photo library or generated embeddings to a VisQ-operated server for routine indexing or search.

Information the App May Access

  • Photos and videos that you allow the app to access through the iOS Photos permission.
  • Locally generated embeddings, descriptions, and indexing metadata used to support search and retrieval features.
  • Basic app state, preferences, and diagnostic information stored locally on your device as part of normal app operation.

How the Information Is Used

  • To index the photos and videos you choose to make available to the app.
  • To generate local retrieval data, match scores, and explainable search results.
  • To store app data needed to reopen the app and preserve previously indexed content.
  • To support optional actions such as saving retrieved images back to your photo library when you request that behavior.

Data Sharing

Based on the current codebase, VisQ is intended to operate without a dedicated remote backend for library indexing and search. The app does not state that it sells personal information, shares photo-library content for advertising, or performs cross-app tracking.

Apple system frameworks may still process information according to Apple's own policies when you use iOS features or the App Store.

Permissions

VisQ requests photo-library access in order to index and search your media. If you do not grant this permission, core library-search features may not work correctly.

The app may also request permission to add images back to your photo library if you choose to save output from the app.

Retention and Control

  • You can change Photos access at any time in iOS Settings.
  • You can revoke consent for photo-library access at any time by changing the Photos permission for VisQ in iOS Settings.
  • You can remove app data stored in the app sandbox by deleting the app from your device.
  • Deleting the app does not delete the original photos and videos stored in your Apple Photos library.
  • If you have a privacy question or want help confirming data removal, contact thawakar.omkar@gmail.com.

Children's Privacy

VisQ is not specifically directed to children under 13. If you believe personal information from a child has been provided through use of the app, contact the developer using the support page.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated as the app changes. When material updates are made, the revised policy will be posted on this page with an updated date.
